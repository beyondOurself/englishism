# Unit 55 get to ... / get home / get to work

## Unit 55

## go to ... go on ... go for ... go -ing

## go to ... (go to work / go to London / go to a concert 等)

□ What time do you usually go to work?

□ I'm going to China next week.

□ Sophie didn't want to go to the concert.

□ 'Where's Tom?' 'He's gone to bed.'

□ I went to the dentist yesterday.

go to

## go to sleep 表示 “入睡”

□ I was very tired and went to sleep quickly.

go home (不带 to) 表示 “回家”：

□ I'm going home now. (而不用 going to home)

## go on ...

<table><tr><td>go on</td><td>holidaya tripa touran excursiona cruisestrike</td></tr></table>

□ We're going on holiday next week.

□ Children often go on school trips.

□ When we were in Scotland, we went on a lot of excursions to different places.

□ Workers at the airport have gone on strike. (= they are refusing to work)

## go for ...

<table><tr><td>go (somewhere) for</td><td>a walk a run a swim a drink a meal</td></tr></table>

□ 'Where's Emma?' 'She's gone for a walk.'

□ Do you go for a run every morning?

□ The water looks nice. I'm going for a swim.

□ I met Chris in town, so we went for a coffee.

□ Shall we go out for a meal? I know a good restaurant.

get on

get 后接名词 (get a letter / get a job 等) 时表示 “收到 / 买到 / 找到某物”：

you get it

you don't have something

you have it

- 'Did you get my postcard?' 'Yes, I got it yesterday.' (= receive)
- I like your sweater. Where did you get it? (= buy)
- Is it difficult to get a job at the moment? (= find)
- (on the phone) 'Hello, can I speak to Lisa, please?' 'Sure. I'll get her.'

此外还有 get a bus / a train / a taxi（乘公共汽车 / 火车 / 出租车）：□ 'Did you walk here?' 'No, I got the bus.'

## get 后接形容词 (get hungry / get cold / get tired 等) 时表示 “变得”：

you get hungry

you're not hungry

you are hungry

□ If you don't eat, you get hungry.
□ Drink your coffee. It's getting cold.
□ I'm sorry your mother is ill. I hope she gets better soon.
□ It was raining very hard. We didn't have an umbrella, so we got very wet.

此外还有：get married（结婚）
get dressed（穿衣）
get lost（迷路）

- Nicole and Frank are getting married soon.
- I got up and got dressed quickly.
- We didn't have a map, so we got lost.

## get to 后接地点时表示 “到达”

☐ I usually get to work before 8.30. (= arrive at work)
☐ We left London at 10 o'clock and got to Manchester at 12.45.

get here/there (不带 to):
□ How did you get here? By bus?
