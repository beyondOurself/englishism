# Unit 91 enough

## enough

I've only got three pounds - not enough for a taxi.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/beyondOurself/englishism/main/assets/egiu/f2b69c01b144cc1f.jpg)


She isn't going to take a taxi.

She hasn't got enough money.

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/beyondOurself/englishism/main/assets/egiu/86341d7a94a980e1.jpg)


He can't reach the shelf.
He isn't tall enough.

## enough + 名词 (enough money / enough people 等):

□ 'Is there enough milk in your coffee?' 'Yes, thank you.'

□ We wanted to play football, but we didn't have enough players.

□ Why don't you buy a car? You've got enough money. (而不用 money enough)

## enough 后也可不带名词：

□ I've got some money, but not enough to buy a car.

(= I need more money to buy a car)

□ 'Would you like some more to eat?' 'No, thanks. I've had enough.'

□ You're always at home. You don't go out enough.

形容词 + enough (good enough / tall enough 等):

□ 'Shall we sit outside?' 'No, it isn't warm enough.' (而不用 enough warm)

□ Can you hear the radio? Is it loud enough for you?

□ Don't buy that coat. It's nice, but it isn't long enough. (= it's too short)

## 记住：

enough + 名词 形容词 + enough

enough money

tall enough

enough time

good enough

enough people

## 可以用：

enough for somebody/something

□ This pullover isn't big enough for me.

enough to do something

□ I haven't got enough money for a new car.

□ I haven't got enough money to buy a new car. (而不用 for buy)

enough for somebody/something to do something

□ Is your English good enough to have a conversation? (而不用 for have)

□ There aren't enough chairs for everybody to sit down.
