# Unit 87 old/older expensive / more expensive

# old/older expensive / more expensive

old older

heavy heavier

expensive more expensive

older / heavier / more expensive 为比较级形式。

其构成形式为 -er (older) 或 more ... (more expensive)。

## older/heavier 等

单音节单词 → -er:

old → older

slow → slower

cheap → cheaper

nice → nicer

late → later

big → bigger

拼写（参见附录5）：big → bigger hot → hotter thin → thinner

以 -y 结尾的单词 → -ier:

easy → easier

heavy → heavier

early → earlier

□ Rome is old, but Athens is older. (而不用 more old)

□ Is it cheaper to go by car or by train? (而不用 more cheap)

□ Helen wants a bigger car.

☐ This coat is OK, but I think the other one is nicer.

□ Don't take the bus. It's easier to take a taxi. (而不用 more easy)

far → further:

□ 'How far is it to the station? A mile?' 'No, it's further. About two miles.'

## more ...

有两个或两个以上音节的单词 → more...

careful → more careful

polite → more polite

expensive → more expensive

interesting → more interesting

□ You must be more careful.

□ I don't like my job. I want to do something more interesting.

□ Is it more expensive to go by car or by train?

## good/well → better bad → worse

□ The weather wasn't very good yesterday, but it's better today.

□ 'Do you feel better today?' 'No, I feel worse.'

□ Which is worse – a headache or a toothache?

than 用于比较级之后 (older than ... / more expensive than ... 等):

□ Athens is older than Rome.

□ Are oranges more expensive than bananas?

□ It's easier to take a taxi than to take the bus.

□ 'How are you today?' 'Not bad. Better than yesterday.'

☐ The restaurant is more crowded than usual.

可以说 than me / than him / than her / than us / than them:

□ I can run faster than him. 或 I can run faster than he can.

□ You are a better singer than me. 或 You are a better singer than I am.

□ I got up earlier than her. 或 I got up earlier than she did.

## more/less than.

□ A: How much did your shoes cost? £50?

B: No, more than that. (= more than £50)

☐ The film was very short – less than an hour.

□ They've got more money than they need.

□ You go out more than me.
