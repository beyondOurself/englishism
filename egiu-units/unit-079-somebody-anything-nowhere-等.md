# Unit 79 somebody/anything/nowhere 等

## somebody/anything/nowhere 等

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/beyondOurself/englishism/main/assets/egiu/5f660f5e76bdb805.jpg)


Somebody (或 Someone) has broken the window.

somebody/someone
指某人，但我们不知道是谁。

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/beyondOurself/englishism/main/assets/egiu/8e6b3297cc4c1879.jpg)


She has got something in her mouth.

something 指某物，但我们不知道是什么。

![image](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/beyondOurself/englishism/main/assets/egiu/822f77bffb88ed80.jpg)


Tom lives somewhere near London.

somewhere 指某地，但我们不知道是哪里。

## 人物 (-body 或 -one)

somebody 或 someone

anybody 或 anyone

□ There is somebody (或 someone) at the door.

□ Is there anybody (或 anyone) at the door?

nobody 或 no-one

□ There isn't anybody (或 anyone) at the door.

□ There is nobody (或 no-one) at the door.

-body 与 -one 意思相同：somebody = someone, nobody = no-one 等。

## 事物 (-thing)

something □ Lucy said something, but I didn't understand what she said.

□ Are you doing anything at the weekend?

□ I was angry, but I didn't say anything.

nothing □ 'What did you say?' 'Nothing.'

## 地点 (-where)

somewhere □ Ruth's parents live somewhere in the south of England.

anywhere □ Did you go anywhere interesting for your holidays?

□ I'm staying here. I'm not going anywhere.

nowhere □ I don't like this town. There is nowhere to go.

## something/anybody 等 + 形容词 (big/cheap/interesting 等)

□ Did you meet anybody interesting at the party?

□ We always go to the same place. Let's go somewhere different.

□ 'What's that letter?' 'It's nothing important.'

## something/anybody 等 + to ...

□ I'm hungry. I want something to eat. (= something that I can eat)

□ Tony hasn't got anybody to talk to. (= anybody that he can talk to)

☐ There is nowhere to go in this town. (= nowhere where people can go)

some 与 any 比较 → Unit 76, any 与 no 比较 → Unit 77, anybody/nothing 等 → Unit 78, everything/-body/-where → Unit 80
